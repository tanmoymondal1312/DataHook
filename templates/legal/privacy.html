DataHook — Privacy Policy

Last updated {{ updated }}

DataHook is a developer tool. You create API endpoints, define the fields they accept, and receive a URL that your own websites and apps can post data to. You then browse those submissions in the Android app and get a push notification for each new one.

This policy explains what the DataHook app and service store, why, and how you can get it deleted.

1. Two different kinds of data

DataHook holds two separate things, and it matters which is which:

  • Your account data — the email, name and settings you give us so you can sign in and manage your endpoints. This is data we hold about you.
  • Your submission data — whatever your own websites, forms and apps post to your ingest URLs. We store it on your behalf so you can read it back. We do not decide what it contains; you do.

Because you choose what your forms collect, you are responsible for the submission data — for telling the people who fill in your forms what you collect, for having a lawful basis to collect it, and for your own privacy policy toward them. DataHook only stores and displays it for you.

Please do not send us data you have no right to hold, and avoid posting special-category data (health, financial or government identifiers) through your endpoints.

Endpoints you create

The endpoint’s name and description, its secret API key, the field definitions you set up, your notification title, and any notification logo you upload.

Uploaded logos are publicly readable. They are served from a public URL so that Google’s notification service can fetch and display them. The filename is randomised and not listed anywhere, but anyone who has the URL can open it. Do not upload anything confidential.

Submissions

For every submission posted to one of your endpoints we store:

  • the data itself — exactly the fields you defined;
  • the IP address the submission was sent from, to help you spot abuse of your endpoint;
  • the time it arrived.

What we do not collect

DataHook contains no analytics, no advertising, no tracking and no crash-reporting SDK. We do not collect your location, contacts, calendar, photos (other than a logo you deliberately pick), device identifiers for advertising, or your browsing activity. We do not sell or rent any data, and we do not use your submission data to train anything.

3. How it is used

  • To sign you in and keep you signed in.
  • To store your endpoints and their submissions and show them back to you.
  • To send you a push notification when a submission arrives — you can turn this off per endpoint.
  • To rate-limit and protect the service from abuse.

That is the whole list. We do not use it for profiling or advertising.

6. How long it is kept

  • Submissions stay until you delete them, or until you delete the endpoint they belong to — deleting an endpoint deletes all of its submissions and fields.
  • Account data stays while your account exists.
  • Device tokens are removed when you sign out, and are also cleared automatically once Google reports a token as no longer valid.
  • Uploaded logos are deleted when you remove or replace them.

7. Deleting your account and data

You can delete most things yourself, at any time, inside the app:

  • a single submission — open your endpoint’s Submissions tab;
  • an entire endpoint, together with every submission and field it holds;
  • a notification logo;
  • your device’s push token — just sign out.

Deleting your whole account

In the app: open Settings and choose Delete account. You will be asked to confirm, and the deletion happens immediately — there is no waiting period and no need to contact us.

Or by email: if you can no longer sign in, write to {{ contact_email }} from the address you signed up with, and we will erase the account within 30 days.

Either route removes the same things: your email, name and password hash, every endpoint you created, every submission stored under those endpoints, any logos you uploaded, and your registered device tokens. Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone, so export anything you want to keep first — the app can export any endpoint to CSV or JSON.

One caveat, for honesty: backups are kept for a short period for disaster recovery and are overwritten on their normal cycle. Uploaded images are not cached by our content-delivery network, so deleting one removes it everywhere immediately.

8. Security

  • All traffic to DataHook is encrypted with HTTPS.
  • Passwords are stored only as salted hashes and are never recoverable.
  • You can only ever read your own endpoints and submissions; requests for anyone else’s return “not found”.
  • Each endpoint has its own secret API key, which you can rotate at any time from the app if you think it has leaked.
  • Sign-in and ingest endpoints are rate-limited.

No service can promise perfect security, but we aim to hold no more than the service needs.

9. Children

DataHook is a tool for developers and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we will remove it.
